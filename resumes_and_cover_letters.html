Craft a Resume that Tells Your Story

Your resume is often your first impression—an opportunity to highlight your unique strengths, skills, and experiences to potential employers. Whether you’re starting from scratch or looking to polish an existing resume, these resources are here to help you every step of the way.

UMSI students are in high demand, and a clear, well-organized resume will help you stand out among applicants. On this page, you'll find practical tips, templates, and guides—all tailored to the needs of School of Information students and aligned with employer expectations.

Resume Writing Made Easy: Steps to Success

  1. Download a UMSI-Approved Template: Start with a format that works—no need to design from scratch.
  2. Gather Your Experiences: List jobs, internships, volunteer roles, coursework, and projects most relevant to your goals.
  3. Write Impactful Bullets: Use action verbs and highlight your skills, results, and unique contributions.
  4. Tailor for Each Opportunity: Customize your resume for every job or internship you pursue.
  5. Review and Edit: Proofread for errors, check formatting, and ask for feedback from peers or a career coach.

Resume FAQ

  • How long should my resume be?
    For most UMSI students and new grads, one page is best. More experienced professionals may require two pages.
  • Do I need a different resume for each application?
    It’s wise to tailor your resume to the requirements and keywords of each opportunity.
  • Is it okay to include coursework or student projects?
    Absolutely! UMSI coursework and projects are valued by employers, especially if they show relevant skills and impact.
  • How often should I update my resume?
    Update your resume every semester, after major projects, or whenever you gain a new role or responsibility.

Write a Cover Letter that Reaches Employers

A well-crafted cover letter complements your resume by providing context, giving you a chance to showcase your enthusiasm and connect with possible employers. Whether you're new to cover letters or looking to refine your approach, these resources will guide you through the process.

UMSI students are known for their strong communication skills, and a tailored cover letter can help you stand out in a competitive job market. On this page, you'll find practical tips, templates, and examples—all designed to help you create compelling cover letters that resonate with hiring managers.

Cover Letter Writing Made Simple: Steps to Success

  1. Understand the Purpose: A cover letter explains why you're interested in the role and how your skills align with the job requirements.
  2. Research the Company: Tailor your letter by understanding the company's values, culture, and recent developments.
  3. Structure Your Letter: Use a clear format with an introduction, body paragraphs highlighting your qualifications, and a strong closing statement.
  4. Use Specific Examples: Illustrate your skills and experiences with concrete examples that demonstrate your fit for the role.
  5. Edit and Proofread: Ensure your letter is free of errors, concise, and professional. Ask for feedback from peers or a career coach.

Cover Letter FAQ

  • Do I always need to submit a cover letter?
    While not always required, a tailored cover letter can enhance your application and demonstrate your interest in the role.
  • How long should my cover letter be?
    Aim for one page, typically 3-4 paragraphs. Be concise and focus on the most relevant information.
  • Should I use the same cover letter for different applications?
    Customize your cover letter for each job to address specific requirements and show your genuine interest in the company.
  • What if I don't know the hiring manager's name?
    Use a general greeting like "Dear Hiring Manager" or "Dear [Department] Team" if you can't find a specific name.
