Networking & Connecting

Build Meaningful Professional Relationships

Networking is a powerful tool for career development, opening doors to new opportunities and collaborations. Whether you're looking to connect with industry professionals, alumni, or peers, this section provides the resources and strategies you'll need to build and maintain meaningful relationships during your time at UMSI and beyond.

Section 1: What is networking and where does it happen?

Networking is the process of building and maintaining professional relationships that can provide support, information, and opportunities throughout your career. It can happen in various settings, including:

  • UCAN (University Career Alumni Network) - A platform where you can have one-on-one conversations with alumni who have volunteered to offer career expertise.
  • LinkedIn - A professional networking platform where you can connect with industry professionals, join groups, and participate in discussions.
  • Immersions - An opportunity that allows students to learn about the industry by visiting an organization's workplace and experiencing a typical workday.
  • Handshake - A career community for students to connect with employers, find job opportunities, and attend virtual events.
  • Career Fairs - Events where you can meet with multiple employers, learn about job opportunities, and make a positive impression.

Section 2: How do I start networking?

Starting to network can feel daunting, but with the right approach, it can become a rewarding experience. Here are some steps to help you get started:

  • Prepare for networking by polishing your resume, practicing your elevator pitch, and generating questions to ask during informational interviews.
  • Identify people to reach out to to build your community.
  • Make contact to ask for a time to connect. Ask for an informational interview, not a job. Informational interviews are about asking professionals questions to learn more about their pathways, their profession, and/or industry, and to get their advice.
  • Follow up with a thank you email within 24 hours of your meeting.
  • Maintain your network by staying in touch, sharing updates, and offering help when you can.

Remember, networking is about building genuine relationships, so focus on connecting with people and learning from them rather than just seeking immediate job opportunities.

Section 3: How do I ask good questions?

One of the best ways to learn about career and potential job prospects is to talk with individuals currently working in that field. This is called “informational interviewing.” An informational interview may be conducted in person, via phone, or through a video call to help you gain knowledge about a field of choice. Here are some tips for asking good questions during an informational interview:

  • Research the person and their role: Before the interview, take some time to learn about the person's background, current role, and the organization they work for. This will help you ask more informed questions.
  • Prepare open-ended questions: Ask questions that encourage detailed responses rather than simple yes/no answers. For example, instead of asking "Do you like your job?" ask "What do you enjoy most about your job?"
  • Focus on their experiences: Ask about their career path, challenges they have faced, and lessons they have learned. This can provide valuable insights into the industry and potential career paths.
  • Inquire about industry trends: Ask about current trends, challenges, and opportunities in the field. This shows your interest in the industry and helps you stay informed.
  • Seek advice: Ask for recommendations on skills to develop, resources to explore, or other professionals to connect with. This can help you build your network and gain further insights.
  • Be respectful of their time: Keep the interview within the agreed-upon time frame and express gratitude for their willingness to share their experiences.

Section 4: What is your digital presence?

In today’s digital age, having a strong online presence is crucial for effective networking. Here are some tips to enhance your digital presence:

  • Manage your online presence: What you post online can be seen by potential employers and networking contacts. Regularly review your social media profiles to ensure they reflect a professional image.
  • Optimize your LinkedIn profile: Use a professional photo, write a compelling headline, and detail your experiences and skills. Join relevant groups and participate in discussions to increase your visibility.
  • Create a personal website or portfolio: Showcase your work, projects, and achievements. This can serve as a digital resume and provide a platform for sharing your expertise.
  • Engage with content: Share articles, comment on posts, and contribute to discussions in your field. This demonstrates your interest and knowledge, helping you connect with like-minded professionals.
  • Maintain your profiles: Regularly update your profiles with new experiences, skills, and accomplishments to keep them current and relevant.

A strong digital presence can significantly enhance your networking efforts, making it easier for professionals to find and connect with you.
