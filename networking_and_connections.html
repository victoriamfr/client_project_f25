Networking & Connecting

Build Meaningful Professional Relationships

Networking is a powerful tool for career development, opening doors to new opportunities and collaborations. Whether you're looking to connect with industry professionals, alumni, or peers, this section provides the resources and strategies you'll need to build and maintain meaningful relationships during your time at UMSI and beyond.

Section 1: What is networking and where does it happen?

Networking is the process of building and maintaining professional relationships that can provide support, information, and opportunities throughout your career. It can happen in various settings, including:

  • UCAN (University Career Alumni Network) — One-on-one conversations with alumni who volunteer career expertise.
  • LinkedIn — Connect with professionals, join groups, and participate in discussions.
  • Immersions — Visit organizations to see a typical workday and meet practitioners.
  • Handshake — Find jobs, connect with employers, and attend events.
  • Career Fairs — Meet multiple employers in one place and explore opportunities.

Section 2: How do I start networking?

Starting can feel daunting; these steps make it manageable:

  • Polish your resume, practice an elevator pitch, and draft questions for informational interviews.
  • Identify people to contact to build your community.
  • Request an informational interview (learn about paths/roles/industries; don’t ask for a job).
  • Follow up with a thank-you within 24 hours.
  • Maintain your network by staying in touch and offering help when you can.

Focus on genuine relationships and learning, not just immediate job leads.

Section 3: How do I ask good questions?

Informational interviews (in person, phone, or video) help you learn from people doing the work.

  • Research them/their org to ask informed questions.
  • Use open-ended prompts (“What do you enjoy most about your role?”).
  • Probe experiences (career path, challenges, lessons).
  • Ask about trends (challenges, opportunities, skills in demand).
  • Seek advice on skills, resources, and other contacts.

Section 4: What is your digital presence?

Strengthen how people find and evaluate you online:

  • Manage your footprint (keep profiles professional).
  • Optimize LinkedIn (photo, headline, experiences; join groups).
  • Build a site/portfolio to showcase work.
  • Engage with content (share, comment, contribute).
  • Keep profiles current with new skills and accomplishments.
